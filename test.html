Visualizing League of Legends Champion Data
This page visualizes champion win rates by patch. (Writeup is at very bottom).

Below graph is a general graph showing win rates with role selection functionality.

Below graph will highlight a specific champion so that when the slider moves the highlighted champion moves with it (shows meta changes/buffs/nerfs).

Below graph will show champion classes between patches (shows champion archetypes that are good or bad in solo queue for each patch).

Below graphs aims to show champions which may be frustrating (high ban rate low win rate).

1. So far, I have made progress on sourcing data, as well as creating two simple initial prototypes for some planned visualizations. The functionality of the first visualization seems to more or less work correctly, and the slider of the second also works. The search bar and highlighting is a WIP. I have a general idea of the other two visualizations I want to implement. I also have a good idea on what I want each graph to look like. I have a skeleton HTML for further implementation. I am also working on a method to scrape further data in python. My current data is from these links:

https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/vivovinco/league-of-legends-stats-s13
https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/vivovinco/league-of-legends-champion-stats
The data from here comes from metasrc.com, a website which includes further data from other patches not included in these two datasets. Potentially, it would be cool to include all available patches in the future.

I want to try my hand at adding a visualization showing the highest winrate champion of a role on a map of the game (so if Urgot is the highest winrate in the top lane, I want to have a little bubble with his pictures pointing at the top lane). Either way, adding animations and making the website look and run smoother is probably going to be the most challenging part of the design. For example, for the highlighted slider, I want the bars to shift smoothly while the slider moves, so that the highlighted bar of the champion can be followed with the eyes. In order to communicate that a champion should be selected, all bars should have their opacity lowered before a champion is selected and highlighted (opacity back to 1).
